## The problem

Every Sentinel alert hitting the SOC queue started the same way: an analyst copy-pasting IPs, domains, and file hashes into VirusTotal, AbuseIPDB, and the internal CMDB — one at a time. Across 80+ daily alerts that is three hours of analyst time per day, before a single decision gets made.

## The fix

A webhook-driven enrichment service, written in Python, that fires the moment a Sentinel analytic rule produces an incident. By the time the analyst opens the alert in the console, context is already posted back — in the alert comments, in Slack, and back into the Sentinel incident entity.

## Architecture

```text
Sentinel (Logic App trigger)
      │
      ▼  webhook (signed)
┌─────────────────────┐
│  enrichment service │  FastAPI, async
│                     │
│  ┌── VirusTotal     │
│  ├── AbuseIPDB      │
│  ├── CMDB (asset)   │
│  └── MISP           │
└─────────┬───────────┘
          ▼
  Sentinel entities ← + Slack (#soc-triage) ← + ServiceNow ticket
```

## What gets enriched

- **IPs** — reputation score, ASN, geoloc, prior incidents
- **Domains / URLs** — VT community score, age, parked/live
- **File hashes** — VT positives, first-seen, sandbox verdict
- **Users** — manager, department, last-known location, MFA status
- **Hosts** — asset owner, criticality, business function, last-seen

## The outcome bullet I actually measured

> Eliminated ~3 hours/day of L1 analyst manual lookup. Mean triage-time-per-alert dropped 30%.

## What I would do differently next time

- Rate-limit VirusTotal calls more aggressively — the free tier is unforgiving under spike load.
- Cache reputation for 1 hour per indicator — most alerts in a burst share the same IPs.
- Sign the webhook (HMAC over body) instead of trusting the Sentinel → Logic App shared secret.
