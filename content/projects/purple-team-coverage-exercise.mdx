## Premise

A purple team that ends with "the red team wins" is a blue team on a bad day. A purple team that ends with *new detections shipped* is detection engineering.

## The exercise

- Offense (partnered red-team) executed selected Atomic Red Team tests targeting Persistence (T1547, T1053) and Defense Evasion (T1027, T1562.001, T1070.004).
- Blue side ran live telemetry capture across Splunk ES, Sentinel, Defender for Endpoint, and Falcon.
- For each TTP: did a rule fire? A hunt surface it? Or was it silent?

## Results

| Tactic           | TTPs replayed | Caught live | Hunt signal | Silent |
| ---------------- | ------------- | ----------- | ----------- | ------ |
| Persistence      | 9             | 5           | 2           | 2      |
| Defense Evasion  | 7             | 3           | 1           | 3      |

## What shipped

12 Sigma rules authored, reviewed, validated in lab, pushed to production, linked to the originating ATT&CK cell in the Detection Coverage Map.

## The engineering discipline this enforces

Purple-team findings that do not ship as rules within 30 days go stale. The exercise created a backlog owner and a weekly rule-ship target. Every silent TTP now has a name, a deadline, and a detection-engineer assigned.
